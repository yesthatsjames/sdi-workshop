Opening Circle
‚è∞ 10 minutes
Welcome participants and frame the workshop as a continental connection, not just a training session. Emphasize that they're building ONE pan-African story from SEVEN cities.

üéØ "Find Your Common Ground" Mixer

3 rounds of connection:

  • Round 1: Find someone from a different region who faces the same issue you do
  • Round 2: Find someone who uses the same solution approach you do
  • Round 3: Find someone whose city has something yours needs
1
Team Formation & Creative Brief
‚è∞ 25 minutes

Segment A: Strategic Team Formation (8 min)

Reveal pre-assigned teams with NO country duplicates. Each team has 4 different countries, at least 2 different regions, mix of Anglophone/Francophone participants.

Segment B: Draw Data Cards & Explore Issues (12 min)

Teams draw one of 8 data cards (Poverty Penalty, Women's Power, Invisible Workforce, Sanitation, Landlords, Jobs, Education, Land Rights). Use Cross-City Questions Framework:

  • What's the HARD DATA on this issue across our cities?
  • What's our LIVED DATA from each city?
  • What's the MISSING DATA we need to investigate?
  • What's COMMON across all our cities?
  • What's DIFFERENT and why?

Segment C: Choose Creative Format (5 min)

Teams select from 7 creative formats designed for cross-city storytelling (see Resources page for full menu).

2
Creative Development with Expert Support
‚è∞ 50 minutes

Sprint 1: Expert Rotations (40 min)

4 specialists (Media, Data, Mapping, LME) rotate between teams. Each expert spends 10 minutes with each team.

Sprint 2: Independent Practice (10 min)

Teams integrate expert advice and finalize their creative piece for presentation.

3
Creative Showcases & Peer Dialogue
‚è∞ 30 minutes

Segment A: Performances (20 min)

Each team presents their creative investigation (5 min per team). Audience members receive role cards (Pan-African Organizer, Communication Strategist, Research Coordinator, Creative Producer) to guide their feedback.

Segment B: "Unity & Diversity" Feedback (10 min)

Structured peer feedback using three questions:

  • UNITY: What connected the cities?
  • DIVERSITY: What honored differences?
  • POWER: What could this create?
4
From Performance to Practice
‚è∞ 35 minutes

Segment A: Country Action Planning (15 min)

Participants regroup by home country to create concrete next steps using the Investigation & Network Plan worksheet.

Segment B: Expert Speed Consultations (7 min)

Each country group gets 1 minute with each specialist for targeted support.

Segment C: "7 Cities Network" Gallery Walk (8 min)

Physical network mapping with colored yarn. Participants connect to cities they'll collaborate with, creating a visual web of the pan-African network.

Segment D: Network Commitments (3 min)

Sign the Network Pledge poster committing to stay connected and share findings.

‚ú®
Closing Circle: "One Story, Many Voices"
‚è∞ 10 minutes

Three Rounds of Sharing

  • Round 1 - City Voices (5 min): Each country shares their commitment
  • Round 2 - Individual Voices (3 min): Popcorn sharing of insights
  • Round 3 - Specialist Voices (2 min): Experts commit to ongoing support

Final actions: Group photo, WhatsApp group creation, certificate distribution

üìã Workshop Worksheets

1. "7 Cities, 1 Story" Foundation Sheet

Used during Phase 1 to capture cross-city patterns

  • Hard Data from your card (statistics & numbers)
  • Lived Data from each of the 4 cities in your team
  • What's COMMON across all cities?
  • What's DIFFERENT between cities and why?
  • Missing Data - What do you need to investigate?

2. Investigation & Network Plan

Used during Phase 4 for country-level action planning

  • Issue being investigated
  • Inspiration from other cities in the workshop
  • What we already know (lived experience)
  • What data already exists
  • What we need to find out (missing data)
  • Investigation methods (mapping, surveys, interviews, etc.)
  • Creative format we'll use to tell the story
  • Language(s) we'll work in
  • First step when returning home
  • Which cities we'll stay connected to
  • How we'll share our findings (WhatsApp, KYC.TV, etc.)
  • Support needed from specialists

3. "Unity & Diversity" Feedback Form

Used during Phase 3 for structured peer feedback

  • UNITY: What connected the cities in this presentation?
  • DIVERSITY: What honored the differences between cities?
  • POWER: What could this investigation create if completed?
