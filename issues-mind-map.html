üí° Key Connections

üîó Everything Connects to Land Rights: Without legal tenure, families can't invest in infrastructure (sanitation), can't prove residence (for jobs/education), and remain vulnerable to landlord exploitation. Land security is foundational.
üîó The Poverty Penalty Multiplies Other Issues: Paying 172% more for water means less money for education, making the diploma trap worse. High utility costs force people into informal work to survive, making them invisible to systems.
üîó Young Women Face Every Issue Intensified: Gender discrimination compounds every challenge‚Äîharder to get formal jobs, more vulnerable to landlord exploitation, face greater safety risks with poor sanitation, excluded from land ownership.
üîó The Jobs-Education Disconnect: Schools teach theory while 85% of work is informal. The diploma trap feeds into the invisible workforce‚Äîeducated youth doing unrecognized work because formal jobs don't exist.
üîó Sanitation = Economic Issue: 2-hour toilet waits mean missing work, losing income, staying poor. Annual cholera outbreaks drain family savings on healthcare. It's not just dignity‚Äîit's economic survival.
üîó Landlords Profit From All Failures: The worse conditions get (no sanitation, no rights, overcrowding), the more profitable slums become. Landlords have no incentive to improve because scarcity drives up rents.
‚úä The Solution: Youth-Led Data Collection: GPS mapping proves land claims. Community surveys expose the poverty penalty. Documenting the invisible economy forces recognition. Creative storytelling turns numbers into advocacy. Data is the weapon that breaks the cycle.
üåç Why "7 Cities, 1 Story" Matters: These same patterns repeat across Dakar, Freetown, Accra, Kampala, Nairobi, Lusaka, and Lilongwe. Cross-city data proves this isn't isolated‚Äîit's systemic. Shared evidence creates collective power for change.
