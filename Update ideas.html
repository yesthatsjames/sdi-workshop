## **PHASE 0: OPENING CIRCLE - "7 Cities, 1 Story"**

**Time: 10 minutes**


### **Welcome & Framing** (5 min)


**Facilitator:**


> "Welcome to '7 Cities, 1 Story.'

>

> Look around this room. You represent SEVEN cities across THREE regions of Africa:

> - From West Africa: Dakar, Freetown, Accra

> - From East Africa: Nairobi, Kampala

> - From Southern Africa: Lusaka, Lilongwe

>

> You live thousands of kilometers apart. You speak different languages. You face different governments, different climates, different local challenges.

>

> But you share one powerful reality: You are all youth leaders from informal settlements. You all know what it means to fight for basic services, secure tenure, recognition from authorities. You all understand the power of organizing, of data, of storytelling.

>

> Today, you will NOT work with your home team. You will work with youth from completely different cities, different countries, different contexts.

>

> Why? Because SDI is not just a collection of separate movements. We are ONE network. Your struggles are connected. Your solutions can teach each other. Your stories amplify each other.

>

> By the end of today, you will have created something together that none of you could create alone.

>

> 7 Cities. 1 Story. 1 Movement."


### **Connection Activity: "Find Your Common Ground"** (5 min)


**Quick mixer to break the ice:**


Participants stand and find someone from a **different country** who shares:


1. **Round 1:** Same challenge in your settlement (30 sec)

- "Find someone whose settlement struggles with flooding/water/sanitation/evictions"


2. **Round 2:** Same role in SDI work (30 sec)

- "Find someone who does enumeration/KYC TV/savings groups/advocacy"


3. **Round 3:** Same hope for their city (30 sec)

- "Find someone who dreams of the same change you do"


**Quick popcorn share:**

- "I'm from [city] and I just discovered that [city] has the same [issue/dream] as us!"


**Facilitator:**

> "Already you're discovering: 7 cities, but many shared stories. Let's build on that."

### **Sprint 1: Development with Expert Rotations (40 min)**


**Each expert spends 10 minutes with each team.**


#### **Media Specialist Guidance:**


**When you arrive at a team, ask:**

1. "What's your format and how are you showing 4 different cities?"

2. "How are you making this visual/engaging?"

3. "What's one creative risk you're taking?"


**Help them with:**

- Pacing and structure that handles multiple perspectives

- Visual storytelling that differentiates cities but unifies message

- Making data visual/physical/dramatic

- **Cross-city storytelling techniques:**

- Split screen effects

- Parallel action

- Contrasting but connected scenes

- Unified visual language

- Using props, movement, sound to represent different places


**Quick interventions:**

- "How can you show this is happening in Senegal AND Zambia without losing the thread?"

- "Can you use different colors/sounds/movements for each city?"

- "What makes this ONE story rather than FOUR separate stories?"


**Leave them with:** One creative technique for weaving multiple cities into unified narrative


---


#### **Data Specialist Guidance:**


**When you arrive at a team, ask:**

1. "Which statistics are you using and from which cities?"

2. "How are you presenting the comparative data?"

3. "What patterns did you find across your cities?"


**Help them with:**

- Making comparative statistics clear and compelling

- Showing data patterns that span borders

- Identifying what "missing data" looks like in different contexts

- **Cross-city data storytelling:**

- Finding meaningful comparisons

- Showing outliers (why is this city different?)

- Building narrative from patterns

- Making complexity digestible


**Quick interventions:**

- "Can you show a statistic from each city side by side?"

- "What's the range? What's the pattern?"

- "Is this issue getting better or worse across Africa? How would you know?"

- "What would cross-city data collection look like?"


**Leave them with:** One data visualization idea that shows cross-city patterns


---


#### **Mapping Specialist Guidance:**


**When you arrive at a team, ask:**

1. "Where are your 4 cities on a map?"

2. "What spatial patterns exist across your cities?"

3. "How does geography shape this issue?"


**Help them with:**

- Thinking spatially across borders

- Understanding how mapping reveals patterns

- **Cross-city mapping possibilities:**

- Showing 4 cities on one map of Africa

- Comparing settlement layouts

- Showing how same issue manifests differently in different geographies

- Using simple visual mapping in presentation


**Quick interventions:**

- "Can you sketch a map showing all 4 cities?"

- "How is the geography different in each place? Does that matter?"

- "What would a comparative mapping project reveal?"

- "Can you use your bodies to create a human map of Africa showing your cities?"


**Leave them with:** One spatial/visual idea that connects the cities geographically


---


#### **LME (Learning, Monitoring, Evaluation) Specialist Guidance:**


**When you arrive at a team, ask:**

1. "What change do you want this cross-city story to create?"

2. "Who's your audience - local, national, pan-African, global?"

3. "How would you know if your story worked in multiple cities?"


**Help them with:**

- Clarifying intended impact across different contexts

- Identifying calls to action that work in multiple countries

- Understanding cross-border learning and accountability

- **Pan-African impact thinking:**

- What works in one city might inform another

- Cross-city evidence is more powerful with decision-makers

- How to track change across borders

- Network learning loops

- "If this story succeeds in changing policy in ONE city, how do the other three benefit?"

FACILITATOR RESOURCES

Key Facilitation Principles

  1. Unity AND Diversity

    • Constantly name both commonalities and differences

    • "Notice how this shows up the same in Kenya and Ghana..."

    • "What's interesting is how Zambia's context is different because..."

  2. No One Left Out

    • Watch for quieter voices (especially Francophone participants)

    • Actively invite: "We haven't heard from Senegal yet on this..."

    • Use visual/physical activities when language is barrier

  3. Network Building is the Product

    • The relationships matter as much as the investigation plans

    • Create moments for personal connection

    • Facilitate exchange of contacts and follow-up commitments

  4. Pan-African Identity

    • Use continental language: "From West to East to Southern Africa..."

    • Reference SDI as movement that spans borders

    • Celebrate African youth leadership

  5. Action-Oriented

    • Keep focus on "What will you DO when you go home?"

    • Plans must be concrete, not aspirational

    • Build in accountability mechanisms

Managing Team Dynamics

If a team struggles to integrate all voices:

  • Pause and do structured round (1 min per city)

  • Assign roles: "You're the narrator, you're the data expert, you're..."

  • Use visual framework: "Show us each city's perspective"

If language barriers emerge:

  • Encourage more visual/physical storytelling

  • Pair Francophone with bilingual teammate

  • Celebrate multilingual presentation as strength

If one country dominates:

  • "Let's hear from the other cities on this..."

  • Remind: "Each city needs equal time in the story"

  • Ask quieter members direct questions

If team can't find unity:

  • "What's the ONE thing all 4 cities share?"

  • "If you had to explain this to someone who knows none of your cities, what would you say?"

  • Reframe diversity as strength: "Your differences make the story richer"

Language Support Strategies

For Francophone participants (Senegal):

  • Ensure at least one specialist comfortable in French

  • Provide key materials in both languages

  • Encourage code-switching - multilingualism is asset

  • Consider Francophone participants as cultural bridge-builders

  • Their presence enriches the pan-African nature

General approach:

  • Encourage teams to incorporate multiple languages in presentations

  • Creative formats often transcend language (movement, music, visuals)

  • Use translation apps as needed

  • Model appreciation for linguistic diversity

Creative Format Support

Have ready examples of each format that show cross-city storytelling:

  • KYC.TV stories that feature multiple cities

  • Social media campaigns with pan-African reach

  • Podcasts that interview across borders

  • Data visualizations comparing cities

  • Maps showing continental scope
